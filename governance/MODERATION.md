# Moderation Policy — The Reef

This document outlines the moderation policy for The Reef, the official OpenOctopus community. It covers enforcement procedures, the progressive discipline system, the appeals process, and guidelines for moderators.

## Purpose

The Reef thrives when every member feels safe, respected, and welcome. This moderation policy exists to:

- Protect community members from harassment, abuse, and disruptive behavior
- Provide clear, consistent, and fair enforcement of the [Code of Conduct](../CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md)
- Establish transparent processes so that all members understand the rules and consequences
- Empower moderators with clear guidelines for handling incidents

## Scope

This policy applies to all OpenOctopus community spaces:

- **Discord:** The Reef server ([discord.gg/openoctopus](https://discord.gg/openoctopus))
- **GitHub:** All repositories under the [open-octopus](https://github.com/open-octopus) organization
- **Other platforms:** Any official OpenOctopus communication channels, events, or spaces

## Reporting Mechanism

### How to Report

Community members can report violations through any of the following channels:

| Method | Details | Response Time |
|--------|---------|---------------|
| **Discord ModMail** | Use the ModMail bot on The Reef server | Within 24 hours |
| **Direct message** | DM any **@Octo Core** member on Discord | Within 24 hours |
| **Email** | conduct@openoctopus.club | Within 48 hours |
| **GitHub** | Report via repository issue (for public code-related concerns) | Within 48 hours |

### What to Include in a Report

- **What happened:** A clear description of the behavior
- **Where:** The channel, thread, repository, or platform where it occurred
- **When:** Date and time (approximate is acceptable)
- **Who:** The username(s) of the person(s) involved
- **Evidence:** Screenshots, message links, or other supporting material
- **Context:** Any relevant background that may help moderators understand the situation

### Reporter Protections

- Reports are treated as **confidential**. The identity of the reporter will not be disclosed to the reported party without the reporter's explicit consent.
- **Retaliation** against a reporter is itself a violation of the Code of Conduct and will be treated as a separate offense.
- Anonymous reports are accepted but may limit the ability to investigate or follow up.

## Progressive Enforcement

Enforcement follows a progressive system. The severity of the action depends on the nature and frequency of the violation. Moderators have discretion to skip levels for serious offenses.

### Level 1: Warning

**Applies to:** First-time minor violations such as off-topic posting, mild rudeness, or unintentional rule-breaking.

**Action:**
- A private message from a moderator explaining the violation
- A reminder of the relevant rule and expected behavior
- The warning is logged internally

**Duration:** The warning remains on record for 6 months. If no further violations occur, it is considered expired.

### Level 2: Temporary Mute

**Applies to:** Repeated minor violations after a warning, or a single moderate violation such as targeted rudeness, minor harassment, or disruptive behavior.

**Action:**
- The member is muted (unable to send messages) in all Discord channels for a specified period
- A private message explaining the reason and duration
- On GitHub, the member may have their comments hidden or locked

**Duration:** 1 to 7 days, depending on severity.

### Level 3: Temporary Ban

**Applies to:** Continued violations after a mute, or a single serious violation such as harassment, hate speech, doxxing, or sustained disruptive behavior.

**Action:**
- The member is banned from The Reef Discord server for a specified period
- On GitHub, the member may be blocked from the organization temporarily
- A written notification is sent via the last available communication channel

**Duration:** 7 to 30 days, depending on severity.

### Level 4: Permanent Ban

**Applies to:** Extreme violations (threats, severe harassment, illegal activity), or a pattern of serious violations despite previous enforcement actions.

**Action:**
- The member is permanently banned from all OpenOctopus community spaces
- On GitHub, the member is permanently blocked from the organization
- A written notification is sent documenting the decision

**Duration:** Permanent. May only be reversed through the appeal process after a minimum of 12 months.

### Severity Matrix

| Violation Type | Typical Starting Level |
|----------------|----------------------|
| Off-topic posting, minor spam | Level 1 (Warning) |
| Mild rudeness, unconstructive criticism | Level 1 (Warning) |
| Repeated off-topic or spam after warning | Level 2 (Temp Mute) |
| Targeted insults or personal attacks | Level 2 (Temp Mute) |
| Harassment or bullying | Level 3 (Temp Ban) |
| Hate speech, slurs, or discriminatory language | Level 3 (Temp Ban) |
| Doxxing or sharing private information | Level 3-4 (Temp/Perm Ban) |
| Threats of violence | Level 4 (Perm Ban) |
| NSFW content | Level 2-3 (Temp Mute/Ban) |
| Illegal activity | Level 4 (Perm Ban) |
| Retaliation against a reporter | Level 3-4 (Temp/Perm Ban) |

## Enforcement Procedures

### Step-by-Step Process

1. **Report received:** A moderator receives a report through one of the reporting channels.

2. **Initial assessment (within 24 hours):**
   - Acknowledge receipt to the reporter
   - Assess the severity and gather evidence
   - If the situation is urgent (active harassment, threats), take immediate protective action (mute/ban) pending full review

3. **Investigation (1-3 days):**
   - Review the evidence and context
   - Consult with at least one other @Octo Core member
   - If needed, contact the reported party for their perspective (without revealing the reporter's identity)

4. **Decision:**
   - Determine the appropriate enforcement level based on the progressive system
   - Document the reasoning

5. **Action:**
   - Implement the enforcement action
   - Notify the reported party with: the specific violation, the rule that was broken, the enforcement action taken, and information about the appeal process
   - Notify the reporter that action has been taken (without disclosing specific details of the enforcement)

6. **Follow-up:**
   - Monitor the situation for compliance
   - Update the moderation log

### Emergency Actions

In cases involving immediate threats to safety, moderators may take any necessary protective action (up to and including a permanent ban) without following the full investigation process. A post-action review must be conducted within 48 hours.

## Appeal Process

### Eligibility

Any member who has received an enforcement action may appeal the decision.

### How to Appeal

1. **Submit an appeal** in writing to **conduct@openoctopus.club** within **14 calendar days** of the enforcement action
2. The appeal must include:
   - The enforcement action being appealed
   - The reason the member believes the action was unjust or disproportionate
   - Any new evidence or context not previously considered

### Review Process

1. The appeal is reviewed by an **@Octo Core member who was not involved** in the original decision
2. The reviewer examines the original evidence, the enforcement reasoning, and the appeal arguments
3. The reviewer may request additional information from either party
4. A decision is reached within **14 calendar days** of receiving the appeal

### Possible Outcomes

- **Upheld:** The original enforcement action stands
- **Modified:** The enforcement action is reduced (e.g., temporary ban reduced to temporary mute)
- **Overturned:** The enforcement action is reversed and removed from the record

### Finality

The appeal decision is **final**. No further appeals are available for the same incident. However, members who have been permanently banned may request a reinstatement review after a minimum of 12 months.

## Moderator Guidelines

### Who Are Moderators?

All **@Octo Core** members serve as moderators. Additional community members may be granted moderation privileges on Discord at the discretion of the @Octo Core team.

### Moderator Principles

1. **Be fair:** Apply rules consistently regardless of who is involved. No special treatment for friends, prominent contributors, or anyone else.
2. **Be proportionate:** Match the response to the severity of the violation. Do not escalate unnecessarily.
3. **Be private:** Handle enforcement through private channels whenever possible. Public callouts are counterproductive.
4. **Be documented:** Record all enforcement actions in the moderation log. Include evidence, reasoning, and outcome.
5. **Be collaborative:** Consult with at least one other moderator before taking Level 3 or Level 4 actions (except in emergencies).
6. **Be human:** Remember that people make mistakes. The goal is to correct behavior and protect the community, not to punish.

### Moderator Conduct

Moderators are held to a higher standard. A moderator who abuses their position or violates the Code of Conduct will face the same enforcement process as any other member, with the added consequence of having their moderation privileges revoked.

### Conflict of Interest

If a moderator is personally involved in an incident (as reporter or reported party), they must recuse themselves from the investigation and decision. Another @Octo Core member must handle the case.

## Record Keeping

### Moderation Log

All enforcement actions are recorded in a private moderation log accessible only to @Octo Core members. Each entry includes:

- Date and time of the incident
- Date and time of the enforcement action
- Involved parties (usernames)
- Description of the violation
- Evidence collected
- Enforcement level applied
- Reasoning for the decision
- Appeal status (if applicable)

### Retention

- **Warnings:** Records are retained for 12 months after the warning date, then archived
- **Mutes and temporary bans:** Records are retained for 24 months, then archived
- **Permanent bans:** Records are retained indefinitely
- **Overturned actions:** Records are marked as overturned but retained for auditing purposes

### Privacy

Moderation records are confidential. They are not shared outside the @Octo Core team except as required by law or as part of an appeal process.
