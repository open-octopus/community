# AMA (Ask Me Anything)

Community-driven Q&A sessions where @Octo Core members, notable contributors, or guest speakers answer questions from The Reef.

## Overview

| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Frequency** | Ad hoc (typically monthly or around major releases) |
| **Duration** | 60 minutes |
| **Channel** | `#general` on Discord (text-based) or `General Voice` (voice-based) |
| **Organizer** | @Octo Core team |
| **Open to** | All community members |

## Purpose

AMA sessions provide the community with direct access to project leaders, domain experts, and notable contributors. They serve to:

- Build transparency around project direction, design decisions, and roadmap
- Give community members a voice in shaping the project
- Share knowledge about AI agents, Realm design, SOUL.md authoring, and related topics
- Welcome guest speakers from adjacent communities or fields
- Create a sense of connection between contributors and the leadership team

## AMA Types

### Project AMA

An @Octo Core member answers questions about OpenOctopus development, roadmap, architecture, or community direction.

**Best for:** Major releases, milestone announcements, or periodic "state of the project" updates.

### Guest AMA

An external guest (AI researcher, open-source maintainer, domain expert) shares their perspective and answers questions.

**Best for:** Cross-pollination with other communities, learning from domain experts, or exploring adjacent technologies.

### Topic AMA

A focused session on a specific technical or community topic, led by the most knowledgeable person on that subject.

**Best for:** Deep dives into Realm design, SOUL.md best practices, LLM provider integration, or specific package architecture.

## Scheduling Process

### Proposing an AMA

Any community member can propose an AMA:

1. Post in `#ideas` on Discord with:
   - Suggested speaker or topic
   - Why this AMA would benefit the community
   - Preferred timing (if any)
2. An @Octo Core member will evaluate the proposal and coordinate scheduling

### Scheduling Timeline

| Step | Timing | Action |
|------|--------|--------|
| Proposal | 3+ weeks before | AMA proposed and speaker confirmed |
| Announcement | 2 weeks before | Event announced in `#announcements` with date, time, and topic |
| Reminder | 1 day before | Reminder posted in `#general` and `#announcements` |
| Question collection opens | 1 day before | Thread opened in `#general` for pre-submitted questions |
| AMA event | Event day | Live Q&A session |
| Summary | 1-3 days after | Summary posted in `#general` |

## Question Collection

### Pre-Event Questions

A question collection thread is opened in `#general` approximately 24 hours before the AMA. Community members can:

- Post their questions in the thread
- React to other people's questions (upvotes help prioritize popular questions)
- Questions can be anonymous — DM an @Octo Core member and they will post it on your behalf

### Live Questions

During the AMA, community members can also ask questions live:

- **Text AMA:** Post questions in the designated thread in `#general`
- **Voice AMA:** Use the text chat alongside the voice channel, or raise your hand to ask verbally

### Question Guidelines

- Keep questions respectful and relevant to the topic
- One question at a time — wait for your question to be answered before asking another
- Avoid questions that have already been asked (check the thread first)
- Both technical and non-technical questions are welcome

## Event Format

### Text-Based AMA (Default)

```
AMA — [Topic/Speaker Name]
Date: [YYYY-MM-DD]
Time: [HH:MM UTC] (60 minutes)
Channel: #general

Format:
1. Introduction (5 min)
   - Moderator introduces the speaker and topic
   - Speaker gives a brief opening statement

2. Pre-submitted Questions (25 min)
   - Moderator presents pre-submitted questions, prioritized by upvotes
   - Speaker responds in the thread

3. Live Questions (25 min)
   - Community members post new questions
   - Speaker responds in real-time

4. Closing (5 min)
   - Speaker shares final thoughts
   - Moderator thanks the speaker and community
   - Links to follow-up resources
```

### Voice-Based AMA

Same structure as text-based, but conducted in the `General Voice` channel:

- Questions are submitted in the text chat alongside the voice channel
- The moderator reads questions aloud and the speaker answers verbally
- Participants can raise their hand to ask follow-up questions
- The session is not recorded unless all participants consent

## Roles During an AMA

| Role | Responsibilities |
|------|-----------------|
| **Speaker** | Answers questions, shares insights, engages with the community |
| **Moderator** | Introduces the speaker, manages the question queue, keeps time, ensures respectful discourse |
| **Note-taker** | Captures key Q&A pairs for the summary |

The moderator is typically an @Octo Core member. The note-taker is a volunteer from the community.

## Code of Conduct

AMA sessions are community events and are subject to the [Code of Conduct](../CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md). The moderator will enforce community standards during the event. Questions or comments that violate the Code of Conduct will be removed, and the author may face moderation action.
