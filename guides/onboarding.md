# Welcome to The Reef

Welcome to The Reef — the official community for [OpenOctopus](https://github.com/open-octopus/openoctopus), the realm-native personal life assistant Agent system.

Whether you are here to use OpenOctopus, build Realm packages, design Entity personalities, contribute code, or simply explore — you are in the right place.

## What is OpenOctopus?

OpenOctopus organizes your life into autonomous domains called **Realms** — each with its own knowledge base, Agent team, and skill set. It provides a **Summon** mechanism to transform real-world entities (pets, people, assets, organizations) into living AI Agents with memory, personality, and proactive behavior.

Think of it as an octopus: the central body coordinates, while each tentacle (Realm) operates independently in its own domain, reaching into different areas of your life.

## The Five Core Concepts

These five terms are foundational to OpenOctopus. They are always written in English, even in non-English contexts.

| Concept | What It Is | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| **Realm** | An autonomous life domain — like an octopus tentacle with its own nerve center | Pet care, finance, legal, health, work |
| **Entity** | An object within a Realm | Your dog (living), your car (asset), your company (organization), a savings goal (abstract) |
| **Summon** | The process of transforming an Entity into a living AI Agent | Summoning your pet into an Agent that tracks vet appointments, food schedules, and health history |
| **Agent** | An AI agent that acts on your behalf — three tiers: Central, Realm, and Summoned | A health Realm Agent that monitors your fitness data, a Summoned Agent for your dog |
| **Skill** | A capability available to Agents — either Global (shared) or Realm-scoped (domain-specific) | Search (global), vet query (pet Realm), tax calculation (finance Realm) |

## Tips for New Members

- **Observe first.** Spend some time reading existing conversations and pinned messages before diving in.
- **Ask questions.** There are no stupid questions. The community is here to help.
- **Be patient.** Not everyone is in the same time zone. Responses may take a few hours.
- **Use the right channel.** It helps conversations stay organized and makes it easier for others to help.
- **Read merged PRs.** If you plan to contribute code, reading 3-5 recently merged PRs is the fastest way to learn the project's style and conventions.
